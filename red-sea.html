The Red Sea shipping crisis, in your grocery bill.

Since November 2023, Houthi attacks on commercial vessels in the Red Sea have forced ships to reroute around the Cape of Good Hope — adding 10–14 days and ↗ $3,000+ per container to shipping costs. This tool maps how those freight and insurance cost shocks flow through container supply chains into the price of 59 everyday foods.

This page was built after conversations with Matilde Ciani — whose questions about global shipping chokepoints pushed it from idea to reality. Part of a broader plan to map every major chokepoint in the world.

Exposure is Pressure, not Price

A 40% exposure does not mean the price has risen by 40%. It means 40% of the cost of that food is currently under stress from global supply chain shocks. Retailers may absorb some of this, or pass it on to you over several months.

About

Tare is a data tool that maps supply chain exposure. In measurement, tare is the weight of the container — what you subtract to find the true weight of the contents.

Freight exposure scores are computed from Drewry World Container Index, Freightos Baltic Index (FBX), and country-level Suez route dependency estimates from UN Comtrade. Exchange rates via Frankfurter (ECB). Updated daily.
